The gap nobody's watching

Your DNS was secure the day you set it up.
It isn't anymore.

DNS risk is a moving target. A bucket gets deleted, a cert expires, a vendor is decommissioned — and a quiet record becomes a live entry point. Point-in-time scanners miss it because the danger appears after the scan.

🪝

Subdomain takeover

Dangling CNAMEs pointing at de-provisioned cloud resources let anyone claim your subdomain — and serve phishing from your own brand.

📨

Spoofable email

Missing or misconfigured SPF, DKIM and DMARC mean attackers can send mail as you. Most orgs find out from a customer, not a tool.

🌀

Silent drift

Nameservers change, DNSSEC breaks, new subdomains appear, certs lapse. Nobody's diffing it — until something breaks in production.

How Gecko works

Scan. Assess. Alert on change.

A clean separation: collectors gather the raw facts, assessors form the judgments, and the engine re-runs on a schedule so you catch drift the day it happens.

Scan the surface

Every record type, full subdomain enumeration, certificates, DNSSEC, zone-transfer probes and exposed services — across your whole portfolio.

→

Assess for risk

Dedicated assessors grade takeover risk, email auth, DNSSEC, open ports, nameserver and CAA config — security, operational and compliance.

Watch & alert

Findings are stored over time and diffed on every run. The moment a new risk appears, you get a Slack, email or webhook alert — not a quarterly PDF.
